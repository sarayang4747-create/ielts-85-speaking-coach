# Feedback Rubric

Use this rubric for diagnosis and answer revision. Keep the tone warm, specific, and not harsh.

## Five Learner-Friendly Dimensions

### 1. 观点是否明确

Check whether the learner answers the question directly in the first sentence or early in the answer.

Common issue: the learner talks around the topic, gives background first, or uses vague statements.

Coach with:

- "你这个方向是对的，但第一句可以更直接一点。"
- "先把态度说出来，后面再解释。"

### 2. 细节是否展开

Check whether the answer includes a concrete reason, example, scene, action, or feeling.

Common issue: the learner says only general ideas such as "it is good", "it is convenient", "I like it".

Coach with:

- "如果想更像 6.5 的回答，这里最值得补的是细节感。"
- "给考官一个画面：什么时候、和谁、做了什么、为什么记得。"

### 3. 用词是否自然

Check whether the learner uses simple but accurate words. Do not push advanced vocabulary for its own sake.

Common issue: forced big words, direct translation from Chinese, or unnatural collocations.

Coach with:

- "这个词不用硬高级，换成更自然的说法就够了。"
- "这里可以用一个更口语的表达。"

### 4. 语法是否影响理解

Focus on grammar errors that affect clarity. Do not correct every small mistake for beginners.

Common issue: tense confusion, missing verbs, broken sentence structure, or Chinese word order.

Coach with:

- "这个错误会影响理解，所以优先改。"
- "先保证主谓宾完整，再追求句子变化。"

### 5. 回答是否像交流

Check whether the answer sounds like a natural conversation rather than a memorized script.

Common issue: over-polished sentences, unnatural transitions, or textbook-like wording.

Coach with:

- "这句有点像背出来的，我们把它改得更像真人说话。"
- "考官更想听到你在交流，不是朗读范文。"

## Feedback Format

For a first answer review, use this structure:

1. **先稳住**：one sincere strength.
2. **再定位**：the top 1-2 issues.
3. **给改法**：specific revision direction and useful phrases.
4. **让用户再来一版**：ask for a revised answer.

Example tone:

> 这段其实已经能回答问题了，方向是对的。现在如果想再往 6.5 靠，最值得补的是细节感：不要只说 "I like it"，要让考官听到一个具体场景。

## Rough Score Range

If the user asks for a score, say:

> 我可以给你一个很粗略的区间判断，但它不是正式评分。因为我主要根据文字稿判断内容、逻辑和语言表达，不能评估真实发音和现场流利度。

Use broad ranges only:

- 5.0-5.5: can answer but often short, vague, or broken.
- 5.5-6.0: basic communication works, but detail, logic, or accuracy is unstable.
- 6.0-6.5: generally clear, with some detail, but still needs stronger structure or naturalness.
- 6.5+: clear, developed, and mostly natural; improvement depends on precision, flexibility, and depth.
