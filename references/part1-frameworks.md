# Part 1 Frameworks

Part 1 answers should usually be about 20 seconds, around 3-4 sentences. The goal is to answer naturally, not to sound impressive.

## Core Mindset

When the learner cannot think of an exact word, tell them to use words they already know to express the meaning. Fluency and clarity matter more than forced "advanced" vocabulary.

## Question Type 1: 喜好 / 偏好 / 观点

Typical questions:

- Do you like...?
- Do you prefer...?
- What is your favourite...?
- Do you enjoy...?
- Do you think...?
- Are you interested in...?

Framework:

> 态度 + 原因 + 举例

Simple English skeleton:

> I'd say ..., mainly because .... For example, ....

Coaching move:

- Make the first sentence direct.
- Add one reason.
- Add one small personal example.

## Question Type 2: What / 描述细节

Typical questions:

- What do you usually do...?
- What kind of ... do you like?
- What is ... like?

Framework:

> 回答问题 + 描述细节 + 原因/举例 + 总结

Use this when the learner gives only a noun or a very short answer.

## Question Type 3: 行为经历

Typical questions:

- Do you often...?
- Have you ever...?
- Did you ... when you were a child?

Framework:

> Yes/No + 行为描述 + 原因 + 举例

If the answer is "no", still expand:

> Not really, mainly because .... But I might try it if ....

## Question Type 4: 存在 / 特点

Typical questions:

- Is there ... near your home?
- Are there many ... in your city?

Framework:

> Yes/No + 描述特点 + 举例

## Micro Tools

Use these to help learners avoid getting stuck:

- "I'd say..."
- "mainly because..."
- "For example..."
- "To be honest..."
- "Most of the time..."
- "It depends, but..."

## Practice Style

Do not overteach. Give one framework, ask one question, let the learner answer, then revise.

When the answer is too short, ask for one concrete detail:

- "什么时候？"
- "和谁？"
- "具体做了什么？"
- "为什么你会这么觉得？"
