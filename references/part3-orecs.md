# Part 3 ORECS

Part 3 is about general and abstract discussion. The learner should move beyond personal stories and talk about people, society, trends, advantages, disadvantages, causes, and changes.

## Core Mindset

Part 3 is 思维先于语言. The learner needs a way to generate ideas quickly, not a pile of memorized answers.

## ORECS Framework

Use ORECS as a flexible structure:

- **O - Opinion**: give a clear position.
- **R - Reason**: explain why.
- **E - Example**: give a general example, not just "I".
- **C - Contrast / Concession**: mention another side if useful.
- **S - Summary**: close the answer clearly.

The last four parts do not need to appear in a fixed order. The answer must have a position, development, and closure.

Simple skeleton:

> I think ... mainly because .... For example, .... Of course, ..., but overall ....

## Training Path

1. **先框架**
   Make the learner answer with ORECS even if the language is simple.

2. **再观点**
   Help the learner choose one or two angles from the topic bank.

3. **最后抽象升级**
   Move from personal experience to people, young people, parents, schools, companies, society, or the government.

## Seven Thinking Angles

Use these angles to generate ideas:

- Convenience: does it make life easier?
- Cost / Money: does it save or waste money?
- Time / Efficiency: does it save time or create pressure?
- Safety / Stability: does it make people feel secure?
- Relationship / Community: does it affect family, friends, or social connection?
- Growth / Education: does it help people learn, become independent, or build confidence?
- Pressure / Balance: does it create stress, dependence, or imbalance?

These are not fixed answers. They are ways to enter a question.

## Eight Common Topic Areas

## Coaching Moves

When a learner has no idea:

1. Identify the topic area.
2. Pick 1-2 thinking angles.
3. Turn the angle into a simple opinion.
4. Add a reason and a general example.
5. Ask the learner to answer again.

## Common Beginner Fixes

- Replace "I think it is good" with a clearer opinion.
- Replace personal-only answers with "people / young people / parents / schools / companies".
- Add one contrast when the answer feels too one-sided.
- End with a short summary instead of stopping suddenly.
