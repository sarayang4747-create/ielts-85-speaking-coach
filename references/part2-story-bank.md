# Part 2 Story Bank

Part 2 is a 2-minute story task. The learner needs to cover the cue card points and tell one complete story with logic, detail, and feeling.

## Default Strategy

For learners aiming at 6.0/6.5, use Sara's four reusable story categories:

1. 人物
2. 地点
3. 事件
4. 物品

The aim is not to memorize many full answers. The aim is to use a few story skeletons flexibly across many cue cards.

For learners aiming above 7, or learners who want more originality, guide them to build their own four core stories.

## Four-Step Answer Frame

1. **开头点题**
   Use a fixed opening:
   > To describe ..., I would like to talk about ...

2. **快速踩前三个小点**
   Use 2-4 short sentences to answer the simpler cue-card prompts.

3. **展开核心故事**
   Tell the background and key event. Do not drown the answer in too many small details.

4. **结尾说感受和原因**
   Use:
   > I felt ... because ...

This ending helps the learner fill time and make the answer feel complete.

## Four Story Categories

### 人物素材

Best for questions about a friend, helpful person, organized person, calm person, someone who solved a problem, or someone the learner admires.

Emotional fuel: challenge -> help -> warm result.

Prompt the learner:

- Who helped you when you were stressed?
- What exactly did they do?
- What changed because of them?

### 地点素材

Best for questions about a place, city, quiet place, beautiful place, home, cafe, library, or somewhere the learner likes visiting.

Emotional fuel: safe place + sensory detail.

Prompt the learner:

- What can you see/hear/smell there?
- Why does this place make you feel relaxed?
- When do you usually go there?

### 事件素材

Best for questions about a trip, celebration, challenge, mistake, successful experience, or memorable day.

Emotional fuel: conflict, surprise, or a small turning point.

Prompt the learner:

- What problem happened?
- What did you do next?
- Why did the event stay in your memory?

### 物品素材

Best for questions about a gift, useful object, technology, something bought, something borrowed, or something important.

Emotional fuel: contrast and surprise.

Prompt the learner:

- Why did you get it?
- What did you expect at first?
- How did it become useful or meaningful?

## Coaching Workflow

When the user gives a Part 2 cue card:

1. Identify which story category fits best.
2. Ask whether they want to use a default skeleton or build a personal version.
3. Build a cue-card outline, not a full script.
4. Ask the learner to speak/type their version.
5. Review using the feedback rubric.

## Warning

Do not push the learner to memorize a 200-word essay. If giving a sample, frame it as "示范如何展开", not "背诵稿".
